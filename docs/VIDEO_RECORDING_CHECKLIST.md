# Video Recording Checklist

Target length: 4-5 minutes, unless the official submission form specifies a stricter maximum.

Suggested pacing:

- First 60-90 seconds: problem, solution, Arc + USDC, and the Credit Passport differentiator.
- Remaining time: working operator, investor, verifier, repayment, and Credit Passport flow.
- Optional longer technical walkthrough may be linked separately.

## Recording Setup

- Use the deployed app.
- Use a clean browser profile if possible.
- Connect a wallet on Arc Testnet.
- Have test USDC ready.
- Have one usable cycle ready for the demo.

## Recording Order

1. Homepage
2. Demo page
3. Market/cycles
4. Cycle detail
5. Operator flow
6. Verifier flow
7. Portfolio/withdrawal
8. Credit Passport
9. Funding routes
10. Submission fit

## What To Say

### 1. Homepage

TradeCycle is USDC milestone finance for SMEs on Arc. Operators create real production or trade cycles, investors fund with USDC, verifiers approve milestone evidence, and repayment is distributed onchain.

### 2. Demo Page

Use the guided workflow to show the complete path: wallet connection, test USDC, operator setup, cycle creation, investor funding, milestone evidence, verifier approval, repayment, withdrawal, and Credit Passport.

### 3. Market/Cycles

Show available cycles and explain that investors can browse cycle terms, capital required, expected revenue where readable, duration, risk controls, and operator history.

### 4. Cycle Detail

Open a real cycle. Explain USDC approval and funding, the cycle-share token investor position, milestone schedule, operator details, and risk controls.

### 5. Operator Flow

Show how an SME operator applies, creates a cycle, submits milestone evidence, releases approved tranches, and repays expected revenue.

### 6. Verifier Flow

Show verifier staking, evidence review, and quorum-based approval before milestone funds can move.

### 7. Portfolio/Withdrawal

Show investor positions and explain that investors withdraw payout after distribution.

### 8. Credit Passport

Show the operator Credit Passport. Explain that it summarizes readable onchain history such as cycles created, capital raised, completed/defaulted cycles, milestone progress, and repayment reliability. State that it is not a regulated credit score.

### 9. Funding Routes

Show that the implemented path is direct Arc USDC. Gateway, CCTP / Bridge Kit, Circle Wallets, and USYC are expansion paths, not implemented claims.

### 10. Submission Fit

Show how the project maps to the selected track, functional MVP requirements, and Arc/USDC stablecoin commerce workflow.

## What Not To Claim

- Do not claim real lending, compliance, underwriting, or regulated financial services.
- Do not claim Gateway, CCTP / Bridge Kit, Circle Wallets, or USYC are implemented.
- Do not claim the Credit Passport is a regulated credit score.
- Do not claim transactions happened unless they are visible or confirmed.

## Closing Line

TradeCycle turns SME working-capital cycles into programmable USDC escrow, verifier-approved repayment workflows, and reusable onchain credit history on Arc.

## Marketplace and Admin demo segment

TradeCycle connects five layers: policy-gated SME onboarding, milestone-controlled USDC working capital, independent verifier quorum, transferable cycle-share positions through an onchain USDC order book, and a reusable SME Credit Passport. The Admin dashboard supports this as the protocol operations and risk-control layer; it is not a replacement for cycle escrow or verifier quorum.

Demo order: problem and solution; operator application and owner-gated review; cycle creation; primary USDC funding; cycle-token receipt; seller lists part of a position; a second wallet partially fills the listing; explain that settlement rights moved with the tokens; evidence submission; verifier quorum; tranche release; operator repayment; current-holder redemption; Credit Passport update; Arc + USDC close. Keep Admin to 20-30 seconds and do not execute sensitive owner operations solely for the recording.
